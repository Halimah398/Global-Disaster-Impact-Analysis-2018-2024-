# Global-Disaster-Impact-Analysis-2018-2024-
This project analyzes global disaster data (2018–2024) using Tableau to examine casualty trends, response times, economic loss, recovery duration, and humanitarian aid distribution. It identifies high-risk disaster types and evaluates how preparedness and response efficiency impact global disaster outcomes.

The primary objective of the analysis is to understand patterns in disaster types, severity, casualties, economic losses, response efficiency, and recovery periods across different countries. It also evaluates how emergency response systems and humanitarian aid influence disaster outcomes globally.

The dataset used for this study includes key variables such as country, disaster type, severity index, casualties, economic loss in USD, response time in hours, aid amount, response efficiency score, and recovery duration in days. These variables were processed, cleaned, and structured in Excel to ensure consistency and reliability for analysis. Independent variables such as country and disaster type were used to examine variations across regions and event categories, while dependent variables such as casualties, economic loss, and recovery time were used to measure disaster impact.

The analysis revealed significant disparities in disaster response and impact across countries. Storm surges, tornadoes, and wildfires were identified as the most deadly disaster types, each accounting for very high casualty levels. Tornadoes and volcanic eruptions recorded the longest recovery periods, indicating severe infrastructure damage and long-term disruption. Response times varied widely across countries, with slower responses generally associated with higher casualties and greater economic losses.

A notable trend was observed in 2020, which recorded the highest global disaster casualties within the study period. Although casualty levels declined slightly in subsequent years, they remained consistently high, highlighting ongoing global vulnerability to disasters. Humanitarian aid distribution showed that countries such as Brazil, Bangladesh, and China received the highest levels of support, reflecting the intensity of disaster impacts in these regions.

Overall, the findings emphasize the importance of strengthening disaster preparedness, improving emergency response systems, and investing in climate-resilient infrastructure. The report provides actionable insights for governments, humanitarian organizations, and policymakers to enhance disaster risk reduction strategies and improve global resilience against future disasters.
